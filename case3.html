Case 3 — Reputation Under Fire: Service Failure Goes Viral

Al-Khair Hospital · Northern Qatar · January 2026 · ASHRM ERM — Strategic Risk Domain

Al-Khair Hospital serves a large expatriate worker population in northern Qatar. In 2024, the hospital launched a Digital Transformation strategy — a new appointment system (MyHMC 2.0) and kiosk-only registration in outpatient areas. The rollout was compressed from 18 months to 6 months to meet a Qatar National Vision 2030 reporting deadline. Staff training lasted just 2 days before go-live. No risk assessment was conducted before launch.

Three weeks after go-live, a video was posted on YouTube showing a 58-year-old Bangladeshi construction worker sitting on the floor outside the hospital, crying. He had no smartphone, no email address, and did not speak Arabic or English. He had waited 5 hours for diabetes medication — he had run out of insulin 2 days earlier.

The video reached 180,000 views in 48 hours. Major Qatari news channels shared it. Arabic and English Twitter hashtags called for MoPH accountability. Advocacy groups submitted formal complaints. The CEO stated: "This was a technology failure, not a leadership failure" — media quoted this immediately.
